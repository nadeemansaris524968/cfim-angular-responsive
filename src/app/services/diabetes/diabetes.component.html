Diabetes Q & A

What exactly is diabetes?

Diabetes is a chronic disease. It impacts how your body processes glucose, which is a type of sugar that serves as a primary source of energy.

When you have diabetes, your body either doesn't produce enough insulin (which is a hormone that regulates blood sugar) or cannot effectively use the insulin it makes. The result is elevated blood sugar levels. If not controlled, it can lead to severe complications.

What are the different types of diabetes?

Type 1 diabetes

This type of diabetes occurs when the body's immune system attacks and destroys the insulin-producing cells in the pancreas. Individuals with type 1 diabetes require insulin therapy to help manage their blood sugar levels effectively.

Type 2 diabetes

Type 2 diabetes is characterized by insulin resistance. Meaning that the body's cells do not respond properly to insulin, leading to elevated blood sugar levels over time. Type 2 diabetes is often associated with lifestyle factors, such as obesity, poor diet, and lack of physical activity.
